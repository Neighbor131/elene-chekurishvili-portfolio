Creatie’s “Generate UI”: A UX Designer’s First Impressions

As AI tools continue to redefine UX/UI design, Creatie’s “Generate UI” feature offers a promising look into the future of design tools. Recently, I had the opportunity to test Generate UI in beta, and I was impressed by how this AI-driven tool intuitively adapts to user needs and simplifies the design process. Here’s a breakdown of its core features, my hands-on experience, and some honest reflections on how it could integrate into existing workflows.

From the start, Generate UI felt like something groundbreaking. Receiving the invitation to test the beta felt thrilling, and it was clear that Creatie’s team is aiming to provide designers with a seamless tool for transforming ideas into functional interfaces. Going in as a first-time user, I approached it with an open mind and a bit of curiosity — how well could it understand my intentions and translate them into usable design elements?

An Overview of Generate UI’s Key Features

Generate UI offers a structured, step-by-step approach that feels welcoming for designers at any level of expertise:

  1. Describe Your Design: This step is all about entering your prompts. The more details you provide, the better the results, allowing the AI to understand the page structure and functional requirements.
  2. Get the First Draft: After clicking “Generate,” the AI processes your prompt and produces a draft, which you can preview in detail. It’s a quick way to see your ideas translated into visual components.
  3. Make Quick Adjustments: If the initial design isn’t quite right, you can provide additional prompts to refine the output. This iterative approach lets you adjust the design without having to start from scratch.
  4. Export the Results: Once you’re happy with the design, Generate UI allows you to export it to Creatie’s canvas for further refinement or to hand off as code. (More export options are promised soon, making this feature adaptable to multiple workflows.)

First Impressions: The Good and the Areas for Growth

Testing Generate UI felt intuitive from the start. The tool’s interface is reminiscent of ChatGPT, making it easy to dive in. One of the standout features is how Generate UI handles design prompts conversationally, offering continued suggestions to refine your ideas. This flexibility is a game-changer because

It doesn’t require you to know specific industry jargon;

you can simply describe your vision, and the AI interprets it. My initial tests involved simply describing the type of interface I wanted. Imagine, for instance, wanting a navigation app screen with specific map-based functionalities.

I just had to describe this conversationally, almost as if I were brainstorming with a teammate.

The AI parsed my instructions and translated them into a preliminary wireframe, providing a base that I could refine. The whole flow below took me only 10–15 minutes.

The customization options — like selecting desktop or mobile resolution, choosing primary colors, adjusting corner rounding, dark/light modes and picking typefaces — allow for a personal touch in each draft. It’s also easy to switch between versions and paste the design directly onto a canvas or export as code, which streamlines the workflow from ideation to execution.

A Practical Tool for Real-Life Scenarios

While Generate UI has impressive potential, integrating it into a large design team already reliant on established tools like Figma could be challenging. For instance, in my team, Figma is deeply embedded in our processes, and transitioning entirely to Creatie may not be feasible. However, Generate UI can be invaluable for quick tasks, like when a designer needs to create a wireframe sample to show stakeholders and is short on time.

It’s also a tool that “speaks your mind” — you don’t need to use industry-specific terms. This makes it accessible for people across different roles in the team, even those who may not have extensive design backgrounds. From my experience, Generate UI is great at understanding general requests and using best practices to deliver a solid design foundation, though it may struggle with highly specific UI details but after further iterations, you still have a very good chance to achieve what you’re looking for.

“For everyone who has an idea but doesn’t know how to properly explain it to the devs”

Honest Critique: What Could Be Improved

One area that could use enhancement is the response time. Occasionally, it took Generate UI around 30–40 seconds to generate a response, which felt lengthy in a fast-paced workflow. An engaging UX solution, such as displaying encouraging messages or useful tips during loading, could help bridge the wait time and keep users engaged.

While Generate UI shows remarkable adaptability, it currently excels more at creating general layouts and adhering to standard and best UX practices rather than producing highly customized, intricate UI elements. It’s an excellent tool for quickly establishing a strong foundation, but designers may still find themselves turning to traditional methods and tools for complex, finely tailored designs.

As a beta product, I didn’t expect it to be flawless, but there were a few aspects I found lacking in the generated designs. For instance, the tool could benefit from pre-styled typefaces and color palettes, as well as ready-to-use components — like a styled CTA button — that would reduce the need to manually create each element. Additionally, the ability to set more than one color during the initial setup phase would make customization smoother and more efficient.

Final Thoughts

First off, I want to thank the Creatie team for giving me the chance to try out Generate UI early. And honestly, huge congrats to them — this is hands-down the best text-to-design/code tool I’ve seen so far. My first experience with Generate UI left me feeling both excited and really hopeful about what it could mean for design. Sure, it has a few quirks, as you’d expect with any beta, but it’s a bold move towards making design more accessible and flexible. For freelancers or small teams, it could be a game-changer, and for larger teams, it’s a solid tool for quick prototyping and brainstorming.

As Creatie continues to refine Generate UI, this tool has the potential to become a staple for designers who prioritize speed, flexibility, and intuitive AI support. It offers a promising glimpse into the future of UX/UI design, where AI isn’t positioned as a threat to designers’ roles, but rather as a supportive partner that enhances creativity and collaboration at every stage of the design process.
