8 Apps & Websites Worth Paying For In 2025: UX Generalist / Product Designer edition

Read on Medium

Last year I wrote about tools worth paying for as a UX/UI designer, and the feedback was quite interesting. So here we are in 2025 — with smarter tools, new design roles, and AI baked into just about everything. If last year was about refining your craft, this year is about evolving your skillset.

Just yesterday, I connected with a group of hand-picked mentors and creatives from the Uxcel community. We exchanged thoughts on how the design landscape is shifting, and one thing became clear:

Designers in 2025 aren’t just designing screens — they’re shaping products, decisions, and strategy.

So whether you’re a solo designer, a product-minded generalist, or just trying to keep up with the tools of tomorrow, here are the ones actually worth your money and attention this year.

Usually, I expect to move on from tools I use frequently and chase new ones, but I’m pleasantly surprised that most of the tools I mentioned last year still remain on my list. Their products continue to evolve, making them worth following.

Uxcel: Your All-in-One Skill Accelerator (Pro membership for $15/year, $24/month)

UX design isn’t just about pixels anymore — in 2025 it’s about product strategy and AI. As a UX/UI & product designer, I know our field is evolving fast. Companies now want designers who understand business, product managers who speak design, and teams fluent in AI. To stay competitive, we need to expand our skillset beyond UX/UI into the realms of product management and artificial intelligence. That’s why I think investing in Uxcel this year will be super beneficial for designer— the platform is evolving right along with the industry. (In fact, Uxcel is launching new Product Management and AI skill tracks this month, and there’s a waitlist to be the first to unlock these new PM & AI skills!)

Dubbed the Duolingo for designers, here are some key features that make Uxcel stand out:
✅ Bite-sized, gamified lessons you’ll actually finish
✅ Interactive skill graph to track your real progress
✅ Shareable certifications (hello, LinkedIn boost 🚀)
✅ A global design community, real career support, and excellent networking opportunities

Whether you’re looking to level up, switch roles, or just stay relevant — Uxcel’s got you covered.

Creatie/Readdy — The Figma Alternatives With Real Generative Power (Starter for $20/m)

I’m sure you’ve tried Figma AI already, or Google Stitch — or, let’s be real, almost every week there’s a new generative AI tool that claims to “build” websites and apps from scratch. While not the newest addition, nothing tops Readdy for me. Objectively, it’s not only one of the most convenient and affordable design generators, but it also teaches you design:

✅ AI-Powered Simplicity — Build and edit your site just by chatting; no tech headaches.
✅ No-Code, No Problem — Drag, drop, and publish — absolutely zero coding required.
✅ Lightning Fast Launch — Go from idea to live website in minutes, not millennia.
✅ Total Customization — Tweak every detail, from colors to content, with ease.
✅ Pro Templates for Every Niche — Ready-made, industry-specific designs for everything from SaaS to dance studios.

Relume: Generate Sitemaps, Wireframes, and Style Guides for SaaS and Marketing Websites (Webflow & Figma Supported, Starter for $18/month)

Relume won’t do your job for you — and that’s a good thing. Thoughtful use is where you can shine as a designer with Relume.

My favorite feature: creating a style guide, which is usually a time sink, especially for juniors. Relume’s AI whips up visual concepts and design systems in minutes. You can merge client ideas, lock in a look, and export everything to Figma or Webflow — no more manual setup. Once you’re satisfied, export components directly to Figma, Webflow, or as React code. Relume’s massive component library (1,000+ strong) is ready to drop into your favorite tools, so you can go from concept to build without missing a beat.

Envato: AI Tools and Flexible Licensing — Perfect for Product Designers (Starter for $16.50/month, student discount available)

We’ve all been there — doing something far from our expertise, whether it’s creating a banner, promoting a post with engaging visuals, updating a portfolio, or hunting for stock images. We’ve worn many hats and dreamed of a single hub for all our creative needs. This year, Envato made it onto my list for a reason.

Here are some of the features you can enjoy:

✅Unlimited Downloads of Premium Assets
With a single subscription, you get unlimited access to over 22 million assets — think stock photos, graphics, templates, fonts, 3D models, and more. No more rationing downloads or hunting for freebies.
✅Full Stack of AI Creative Tools
Envato’s Gen AI suite (ImageGen, VideoGen, MusicGen, VoiceGen, and ImageEdit) lets you generate images, videos, music, and even voiceovers from text prompts. It’s like having a creative sidekick that never sleeps — ideal for rapid prototyping and moodboarding.
✅Lifetime Commercial License
Every asset comes with a lifetime commercial license, so you can use them in client projects, product mockups, or even that side hustle you started at 2 a.m. — without worrying about legal headaches.
✅Diverse Asset Categories in One Place
From UI kits and presentation templates to 3D renders and sound effects, Envato covers every creative need. This means less time switching between platforms and more time actually designing.
✅High-Quality, Curated Content
Assets are created by global artists and regularly updated, so you’re not stuck with the same tired icons or templates. Plus, the quality bar is high — no pixelated logos or cringe-worthy stock photos.

DivRIOTS + Figr: The Ultimate Stack for Figma-Native Designers (divRIOTS $18/month; Figr Pro $79/quarter)

Let’s talk about Figma. Whether we like its monopoly or not, we’re bound to it for a long time. With their latest rollouts, we’ll be working in Figma for the foreseeable future, so let’s check out the plugins worth investing in this year. DivRIOTS stands out for its powerful Figma plugins, seamless file imports, and AI image editing.

No exaggeration — they deliver what they promise. Want to bring a website you like into Figma with autolayout, variables, and styles applied with real content and at your desired resolution? Say no more: the html.figma plugin does just that. Not a frequent user? No worries — you get 10 free credits/exports per month! The pro plan offers:

✅Unlimited imports (fair use)
✅High-resolution images
✅Multiple URLs or .h2d files at once
✅Re-import URLs in one click
✅Premium support

And that’s just one plugin — they have a powerhouse stack.

Speaking of ultimate Figma plugins, I can’t miss Figr — AI for product thinkers. “Design with context, not guesswork” — that’s their hero banner, and that’s exactly what they deliver.

✅Context-Driven Design: Figr lets you feed your research, insights, and product context directly into the design process, ensuring your work is always grounded in real user needs and business goals.
✅AI-Assisted Wireframing and Interface Creation: Generate user flows, wireframes, and interfaces quickly with the help of AI, reducing repetitive tasks and freeing up time for creative problem-solving.|
✅Automated Design System Generation: With the Identity tool, you can create robust, production-ready design systems in minutes — no more tedious manual setup.
✅Design System Audit and Consistency Checks: Nexus, Figr’s audit plugin, helps you find and fix inconsistencies in your design system, ensuring a polished and unified product experience.
✅Curated Brand Strategy Resources: Brandfolio offers inspiration and practical insights by curating brand assets and strategies from top companies, helping you stay ahead of trends and best practices.
✅Faster Iteration and Prototyping: By automating the heavy lifting, Figr allows you to iterate and test ideas at an unprecedented speed, making it easier to refine and perfect your designs.

This year, they are introducing Figr AI — early access is open, and I’m excited to see what’s next.

Mobbin: A Goldmine for Product Designers Seeking Inspiration, Efficiency, and Best Practices (Pro $10/year or $15/month)

Some apps you always return to, no matter what — that’s Mobbin for me. I’ve used it for more than three years; it’s become my encyclopedia. My colleague once joked, “Do they pay you for promoting them?” I just don’t like gatekeeping, so I’ll always advocate for it. Here’s what makes Mobbin stand out:

✅Massive UI Inspiration Library
Mobbin curates thousands of real-world app and web UI screenshots, so you can see how top products solve design problems. It’s like Pinterest, but for people who know what a dropdown menu is.
✅Up-to-Date Patterns
You get the latest and greatest design patterns from leading apps (think Airbnb, Stripe, Notion), so you’re not referencing that 2015 “modern” login screen. Stay fresh, stay relevant.
✅Filter by Flow, Element, or Platform.
Need to see just onboarding flows for finance apps? Or how Shopify handles pricing pages? Mobbin’s filters let you slice and dice by screen type, UI element, app category, and platform (web, iOS, Android). It’s like a design buffet — minus the questionable potato salad.
✅Save Time on Research
Why reinvent the wheel (or the hamburger menu)? Mobbin lets you quickly benchmark, compare, and gather references, so you can focus on actual design work instead of endless Googling.
✅Learn from the Best
See how industry leaders structure their flows, handle edge cases, and solve tricky UX problems.
✅Collaboration & Sharing
You can save collections, share them with your team, and even annotate screens. Perfect for workshops, design critiques, or just showing off your “look how organized I am” skills.
✅Great for Presentations & Stakeholder Buy-In
Need to convince someone that your design isn’t just “vibes”? Pull up Mobbin references to show real-world examples and back up your decisions. (This saved me so much time)
✅Cross-Disciplinary Inspiration
With examples from productivity, finance, shopping, and more, you can cross-pollinate ideas from different industries. Who says a tattoo booking flow can’t learn from a fintech onboarding?

These tools didn’t just save me time — they pushed me forward.

🧠 Bonus for Budget-Conscious Designers

Dia Browser — Built-In AI Assistant Without the Price Tag

If you can’t (or don’t want to) pay for ChatGPT Pro, Dia Browser is a surprisingly powerful alternative. It bakes an AI assistant right into your browser, so you can:

✅Summarize pages
✅Generate content
✅Answer questions
✅Even debug code or assist with research

It’s not as advanced as latest GPT models, but for casual tasks and creative work, it’s more than enough — and free.

Perfect for designers who want AI at their fingertips without the monthly subscription.

If you’re not Arc user, here’s the invitation to Dia for you!

2025 belongs to the designers who adapt, automate, and evolve. Invest in tools that make you better, not just faster.
