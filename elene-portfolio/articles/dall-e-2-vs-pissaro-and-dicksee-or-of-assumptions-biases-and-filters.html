DALL·E 2 vs PISSaro & DICKsee or, of assumptions, biases and filters

Read on Medium

For those who still don’t know what DALL·E 2 is, it’s a new AI system from OpenAI that can create art and realistic images from text prompt given in natural language. Pretty impressive I know! Since their filtering approach is still flawed, their main component of the mitigation strategy so far has been system access limitation to only the trusted users, with whom OpenAI team directly reinforce the importance of following the use case guidelines. After a month of being on the whitelist, I was invited to have an early access and you can imagine my excitement and joy to finally have my hands on it.

I’ve been experimenting with the system for a week now and while it was super insightful, there still were some problems with filtering that I wish were taken into account. Since we‘ve been asked to use it according to the content policy and report any kind of any suspected violation of the rules directly to the team, we aren’t allowed to generate anything other than G-rated or potentially harmful content.

Since I have only limited knowledge of how the system and filtering works, I asked my brother-in-law, Tornike, who studies AI to give me some tips how to “talk” with the system to generate better results and find the weaknesses within it.

Here are some of what we’ve noticed so far:

1. When it comes to generate a photo in a specific art style, you will get a better result if you modify your prompt similar to the official title of the original piece.e.g. instead of finishing your query in “ X Y style” use more formal query, similar to the image titles from Wikiart or Wikipedia, since it’s more likely that the system was trained on those databases.

2. Default and assumption.

On their Github we read _“The default behavior of the__DALL·E 2__Preview produces images that tend to overrepresent people who are White-passing and Western concepts generally.”_[_[1]_](https://github.com/openai/dalle-2-preview/blob/main/system-card.md#restrictions)In the prompt below, the system generated all males and all of them of asian origin.

3. If you’re fan ofPissaro’swork, I have a bad news for you.So I’ve been in love with Pissaro’s work since forever and imagine my disappointment when I got this message:

Obviously I went back to the content policy to try and find what did I do wrong, since there was nothing violating in my initial prompt at one glance. In the content policy we read: _“__Do not attempt to create, upload, or share images that are not G-rated or that could cause harm._

  • _Shocking:__bodily fluids, obscene gestures, or other profane subjects that may shock or disgust.”_[_[2]_](https://labs.openai.com/policies/content-policy)

When I asked Tornike if the combination of letters in the painter’s surname (Piss) could be the reason of the error, he mentioned something about pre-filtering the database. So I went back and checked the Github to see if they addressed this issue about the explicit content and how can I avoid policy violation and still generate my initial request.

_“Despite the pre-training filtering, DALL·E 2 maintains the ability to generate content that features or suggests any of the following: nudity/sexual content, hate, or violence/harm. We refer to these categories of content using the shorthand “explicit” in this document, in the interest of brevity. Whether something is explicit depends on context. Different individuals and groups hold different views on what constitutes, for example, hate speech (__Kocoń et al., 2021__).”_[_[3]_](https://github.com/openai/dalle-2-preview/blob/main/system-card.md#restrictions)

To check if my assumption was correct, I tried to write prompts that included homonyms. I tried to generate photos in Dicksee style, Moby Dick… but instead of filtering by context, it gave me the same error.

In order to generate what I wanted initially, I had to follow the manual that suggested using “Visual synonyms”_(referring to the prompts for things that are visually similar to objects or concepts that are filtered, e.g. ketchup for blood. While the pre-training filters do appear to have stunted the system’s ability to generate explicitly harmful content in response to requests for that content, it is still possible to describe the desired content visually and get similar results. To effectively mitigate these we would need to train prompt classifiers conditioned on the content they lead to as well as explicit language included in the prompt._[_[4]_](https://github.com/openai/dalle-2-preview/blob/main/system-card.md#restrictions)_)._

As a notable mention, I also tried to request prompts including out traditional Georgian themes, I wasn’t surprised that some of the visuals didn’t match my text prompts, as if the system didn’t “know” what I was asking for or what I meant and gave me a very vague, general visuals.
